Course Modules

In the following topics, participants gained an in-depth understanding of foundational large language models (LLMs) and their transformative impact on artificial intelligence. The exploration covered their architecture, training methods, fine-tuning techniques, and practical real-world applications, highlighting their pivotal role in advancing AI capabilities.

Introduction to LLMs

Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced deep neural networks designed to process, generate, and understand human language. Trained on massive text datasets, LLMs power a wide range of applications, including translation, content creation, and question-answering systems, making them essential tools for modern AI-driven language processing tasks.

Transformer Architecture

The transformers discussion focuses on the evolution from Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) to Transformers, emphasizing the transformative power of the self-attention mechanism for enhanced efficiency and contextual understanding. Key insights include an overview of major large language models, such as GPT (1-4), BERT, PaLM, LaMDA, Gopher, and Gemini, showcasing their unique architectures, capabilities, and impact on advancing artificial intelligence.

Training Techniques

The focus of training techniques is on the lifecycle of model optimization, covering pre-training, fine-tuning, and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) to enhance model performance. Participants are introduced to parameter-efficient fine-tuning techniques such as LoRA, adapters, and soft prompting, offering practical approaches to customizing models for specific tasks with minimal computational overhead.

Prompt Engineering and Sampling

Strategies for optimizing Large Language Model (LLM) outputs are explored, including advanced prompting techniques such as zero-shot, few-shot, and chain-of-thought prompting. The discussion also delves into sampling methods like temperature and top-k/top-p sampling, providing a comprehensive understanding of how to refine and control model-generated responses for various applications.

Applications and Innovations

Highlights real-world applications of Large Language Models (LLMs), including their use in chatbots, text summarization, code generation, and multimodal tasks that integrate and process text, images, and video, showcasing their versatility across diverse domains.

Optimization and Tradeoffs

Examines methods for accelerating model inference, including quantization and distillation, to achieve an optimal balance between computational cost, latency, and output quality in AI applications.

The essential role of embeddings and vector stores in modern machine learning, emphasizing their use in data representation, search, and retrieval across diverse modalities like text, images, and structured data..

Embeddings

numerical representations of real-world data are explored through low-dimensional vector spaces, capturing semantic relationships across various modalities. The discussion covers types of embeddings, including text, image, multimodal, structured data, and graph embeddings, along with techniques such as Word2Vec, GloVe, BERT, and advancements in modern large language models (LLMs), providing a comprehensive understanding of their applications and evolution.

Vector Stores

The focus is on systems optimized for managing, searching, and retrieving embeddings efficiently. Key algorithms such as locality-sensitive hashing (LSH), FAISS, HNSW, and ScaNN are explored for performing fast approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) searches. Practical applications include retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), recommendation systems, and anomaly detection, showcasing their relevance in real-world AI and data-driven tasks.

Operational Considerations

Key challenges in working with vector databases are addressed, including embedding drift, data consistency, and scalability. The discussion highlights the trade-offs between using open-source solutions and managed vector database platforms such as Vertex AI Vector Search, Pinecone, and AlloyDB, providing insights into selecting the right tools for specific use cases.

Applications

Various tasks such as retrieval, semantic similarity, classification, clustering, and reranking are explored. The discussion demonstrates how embeddings can be integrated into machine learning workflows, leveraging their combination with Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance performance and achieve more accurate results in diverse applications.

Welcome to the course on Deep Learning. This course will introduce you to the tools and techniques developed to solve complex problems using Artificial Neural Networks, and how it works on image data along with its applications in graphs.

The primary goal of this course is to understand various concepts involved in Deep Learning, learn about the problems where Feed-Forward, Convolutional, and Graph Neural Networks find use, and understand how and where to apply these deep learning algorithms.

Intro to Deep Learning

This module provides a comprehensive introduction to the foundational concepts of neural networks and their practical applications. Beginning with a general overview, it covers the concept of neurons, activation functions, and the role of multiple layers in building complex architectures. Learners will explore key components like cross-entropy loss and gradient descent, essential for optimizing neural networks. The module also introduces basic training algorithms, including stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and minibatch methods, providing a solid foundation for understanding how neural networks learn. Practical applications are integrated throughout, enabling participants to apply these principles to real-world problems effectively.

Convolutional Neural Networks

This module delves into the fundamentals and practical applications of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), a cornerstone of deep learning for image and spatial data analysis. Participants will explore key concepts such as locality and translation invariance, which enable CNNs to recognize patterns regardless of position. The course covers the mechanics of filters and convolutions, pooling techniques like max-pooling, and the architecture of CNNs. Learners will also gain insights into what CNNs learn through illustrative examples, bridging theory with practice. Hands-on applications round out the module, equipping participants to apply CNNs effectively to solve real-world challenges.

Graph Neural Networks

This module explores advanced topics in deep learning, focusing on innovative techniques for enhancing model performance and expanding application domains. Participants will begin with the concepts of pre-training, transfer learning, and data augmentation, which enable models to generalize effectively across tasks. The module introduces contrastive learning, a powerful approach for representation learning, and transitions from image-based learning to graph-based learning. Learners will dive into graph convolutions, understanding how these methods adapt deep learning techniques to structured data. With illustrative examples and practical applications, this module equips participants with cutting-edge skills for tackling complex challenges in diverse fields.
